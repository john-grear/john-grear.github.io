During the Spring of 2022, my team and I collaborated on a group project for the Usability Engineering course at BGSU. Our primary focus was on enhancing the app's usability rather than adding new functionalities.

The app aimed to facilitate the creation of personalized Dungeons & Dragons (D&D) characters, offering users an extensive range of options for character customization. From crafting custom classes and selecting / uploading images to adding unique lore, users could tailor every aspect of their characters. The app allowed users to save different profiles for various characters, making it ideal for managing characters across multiple campaigns.

Thanks to its extensive customization features, the app was not limited to D&D alone. It would be equally useful for tabletop RPG games like Pathfinder, Call of Cthulhu, and more. The project was an interesting exploration of usability principles and would've proved to be a versatile tool for role-playing game enthusiasts, if we were ever to be completed.
