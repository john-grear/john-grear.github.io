I'm intrigued by the idea that concepts we often consider universal, like numbers and counting systems, are actually human creations. While quantities exist in reality, our methods of representing them might not always perfectly align. For example, when adding decimals like 0.3333 + 0.3333 + 0.3333, the total doesn't precisely reach 1. Using rounding, designed for whole numbers, might also not accurately represent decimal values, impacting their precision.
